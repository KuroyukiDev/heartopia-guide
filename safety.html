🛡️ The "3s Config" Safety Plan

In Heartopia, the "3s Config" Safety Plan (Safety, Space, and Settings) — created by the veteran gamer "Wolfiechan" — involves managing how "randos" can interact with your physical home, your character, and your social feed. Since if you're a F2P player, these settings are your primary defense in public channels.

The 3s Config: Menu Paths for Safety

The most critical safety settings are found in the Home Setting tab, accessed via the in-game "Smartwatch."

  • Locking Home/Doors: Smartwatch (Phone icon) > Settings > Home Setting tab
    Friends Only Self Only

    Action: Locate permissions for Furniture Use, Vehicle Use, and Stove/Workbench.

  • Blocking/Reporting Randos: Tap Player Bubble OR click icon on Map
    Report/Block

    Action: Select the Report or Block icon in their profile popup.

  • Restricting Friend Requests: Smartwatch > Settings > Social/Privacy
    Toggle Off

    Action: Disable "Allow Random Friend Requests" to restrict access via UID only.

Shared Strategy: Family Safety Plan

Share this outline with your gaming buddies to help them secure their kids' experience in Heartopia.

1. Space

  • Vehicle Use

    Self Only
    Prevents players from joyriding in your car.

  • Home Privacy

    Friends Only
    Restrict Furniture, Cooking, and Crafting.

  • Boundaries

    Friends Only
    Toggle Visit Permission for the housing plot.

2. Social

  • Friend Requests:

    Adding friends costs 1 Wishing Star to prevent spam.

  • Chat Safety:

    Use the Coffee Umbrella for a temporary 30-min chat. If a rando is toxic, tap their profile bubble and select Block instantly.

  • Anonymous Mode:

    Check Privacy tab to hide online status/location.
Gaming Veteran Tip

Hide your UID from public chat. Only exchange UIDs via secondary communication (Discord/Text) to ensure you only add known friends.

3. Safety

  • Channel Hopping:

    Go to Title Screen > Start to re-roll a new channel.

  • Block & Report:

    Use the "Three Dots" icon for immediate silence.

  • UID Privacy:

    Only share UIDs in private messages.
